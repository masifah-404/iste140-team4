Lore & Worldbuilding

The Cataclysm

Fifty years ago, the continent of Eldoria was whole and prosperous. Seven great kingdoms ruled in relative peace, their combined magical prowess unmatched. But ambition and mistrust led the court wizards to attempt the Ritual of Eternity a spell meant to grant immortality to their lineage.

The ritual went catastrophically wrong. Reality itself fractured, tearing Eldoria into floating fragments separated by a chaotic void known as the Shimmer. Millions died in an instant. The survivors found themselves isolated on islands of land, separated from loved ones and resources.

Now, the world struggles to rebuild. Some fragments have prospered, while others have descended into barbarism. New nations have formed, and ancient evils long buried have awakened in the chaos.

Timeline of Major Events

Year 0 – The Cataclysm

The Ritual of Eternity fails. Eldoria shatters into countless fragments. An estimated 60% of the population perishes instantly.

Years 1–10 – The Dark Decade

Chaos and survival. Fragments isolated from one another. Warlords and monsters emerge. The old kingdoms fall completely.

Years 11–25 – Age of Exploration

Brave souls discover methods to traverse the Shimmer. Trade routes slowly re-establish. New alliances form among the fragments.

Years 26–40 – The Reclamation

Organized nations emerge from the strongest fragments. The Free Cities form. The Ironfell Clans unite. The Verdant Covenant is established.

Years 41–50 – Present Day

Unsteady stability. Political tensions rise. Strange magical phenomena increase. Ancient ruins yield powerful and dangerous artifacts.

Major Factions & Locations

The Free Cities

A democratic alliance of seven city-states built on trade and innovation. They reject the old monarchies and embrace progress, sometimes at the cost of tradition.

Key City: Nexus Prime — a bustling metropolis where all peoples and ideas converge.

Ironfell Dominion

Dwarven clans and their allies who have carved out a mountain fortress. Militaristic and isolationist, they hoard pre-Cataclysm technology and magic.

Capital: Deepforge — an underground city of marvels and military might.

The Verdant Covenant

Druids, rangers, and nature-worshippers who believe the Cataclysm was nature's revenge. They seek balance between civilization and the wild.

Sacred Site: The Heartwood — an ancient forest fragment where nature magic runs strongest.

The Crimson Wastes

Lawless territories controlled by warlords, criminals, and desperate survivors. Dangerous, but opportunities abound for those brave or foolish enough.

Notable Location: Redhaven — A pirate port where anything can be bought for the right price.

The Shimmer

The chaotic void between fragments. Time and space behave strangely here. Travelers report visions, time distortions, and encounters with impossible creatures.

Danger Level: Extreme — Only the most skilled navigators survive passage.

The Fallen Throne

Ruins of the old capital, now partially submerged in the Shimmer. Said to contain the greatest treasures and darkest secrets of the pre-Cataclysm world.

Status: Largely unexplored — most expeditions never return.
